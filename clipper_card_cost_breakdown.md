## 🖥️ Slide 2: What is a Clipper Card & Why We Changed the Plan
* **Slide Title:** Transit Debit Cards vs. Flat Budgets
* **Visuals:** Split screen. On the left: definitions of Clipper cards. On the right: a stat card comparing the $30 budget from 2025 (IMPRINT) with the 4-day travel requirement of 2026.
* **Speaker Script:**
  > "First, a quick refresher. What is a Clipper card? It behaves exactly like a transit debit card. You load it with money, hand it to a scholar, and they tap it at the fare gates. Balances stay on the card until spent. 
  > 
  > You might ask, why not just use the $30 flat budget from last year's IMPRINT program? In 2025, our scholars only had one single day of travel on BART, so $30 covered that round trip and Muni with a massive buffer left over. This year is different. We have multiple travel days across two weeks. A flat $30 load would lead to cards running out of money mid-week, resulting in gate lockouts and travel disruptions. We need a more targeted approach."

## 🖥️ Slide 3: Why 14 Cards? The Mathematics
* **Slide Title:** Why 14 Cards for 24 Travelers?
* **Visuals:** Three stats blocks showing: Cohort 1 size (10), Cohort 2 size (14), and Total Cards Purchased (14). A highlighted callout box explaining the handoff formula.
* **Speaker Script:**
  > "Let's talk about the math. We have 24 total travelers: 10 in Cohort 1 and 14 in Cohort 2. Buying 24 physical cards would mean paying 24 activation fees and leaving huge cash balances stuck on 10 cards that will never be used again once Cohort 1 departs.
  > 
  > Instead, we purchase only 14 cards total. Cohort 1 is smaller and travels only once. Cohort 2 is larger and travels three times. We reuse all 10 cards from Cohort 1 to cover 10 of our Cohort 2 scholars. We only buy 4 additional cards to cover the remaining Cohort 2 scholars (14 minus 10 equals 4). This smart reuse immediately saves us on card stock fees and prevents wasted card balances."

## 🖥️ Slide 4: The Handoff Timeline
* **Slide Title:** Handoff Logistics & Timeline
* **Visuals:** A timeline diagram displaying three phases: Phase 1 (Cohort 1 JLABS travel, Jul 19–21), Handoff Phase (Vault & Sort on Jul 26), and Phase 2 (Cohort 2 travel, Jul 27–29). A red warning banner about the importance of recovery.
* **Speaker Script:**
  > "Executing this handoff successfully comes down to operations. Here is our timeline: 
  > Before Cohort 1 arrives, we load 10 cards with $55. Cohort 1 scholars will use these cards for their JLABS tour on Tuesday, July 21. 
  > 
  > The critical logistical step happens on Saturday, July 26. We must collect all 10 cards back from Cohort 1 scholars before they head to the airport. At this point, each card will have exactly $42.70 remaining on it. 
  > 
  > On Sunday, July 27, when Cohort 2 arrives, we distribute those 10 collected cards to 10 of our new scholars, and hand out 4 fresh cards—which we pre-loaded with $41—to the remaining 4 scholars. Everyone is now fully funded, and we didn't have to spend a single extra dollar reloading the reused cards."

## 🖥️ Slide 5: Why Load Different Amounts?
* **Slide Title:** Why Load Different Amounts?
* **Visuals:** Comparison panels for the two card pools. A clear table showing destinations, Clipper fares, and checkmarks for which card covers which trip:
  * JLABS SSF (Cohort 1): $12.30 RT (Dual pool only)
  * Frank Residences (Cohort 2): $11.70 RT (Both pools)
  * Institute on Aging (Cohort 2): $10.60 RT (Both pools)
  * Silverado Belmont Hills (Cohort 2): $13.90 RT (Both pools)
* **Speaker Script:**
  > "Now, why are we loading different amounts on the cards? Why is one pool $55 and the other $41?
  > 
  > The 10 dual-cohort cards are active during both weeks. They have to cover Cohort 1's JLABS trip ($12.30) plus Cohort 2's three site visits ($36.20). That's a total of $48.50 in fares. Loading $55 gives these scholars a $6.50 safety net for missed taps or fare adjustments.
  > 
  > The 4 Cohort-2-only cards are brand-new and only need to cover Cohort 2's three trips. They bypass the JLABS day entirely. Their total expected fare spend is $36.20. Loading $41 on them gives them a $4.80 buffer. By loading $41 instead of $55 on these 4 cards, we save $56 in unnecessary upfront budget."

## 🖥️ Slide 6: Cost Summary & Grand Total
* **Slide Title:** Interactive Budget Calculator & Summary
* **Visuals:** Comprehensive breakdown table of the grand total budget:
  * 10 Dual-Cohort Cards @ $55.00 = $550.00
  * 4 Cohort-2-Only Cards @ $41.00 = $164.00
  * Subtotal Loaded Funds: $714.00
  * Physical Card stock fee (14 cards @ $3.00) = $42.00
  * Grand Total (Physical): $756.00
  * Grand Total (Mobile Wallets): $714.00
* **Speaker Script:**
  > "Let's summarize the overall financial picture. The total funds loaded across all 14 cards will be $714.00. 
  > 
  > If we purchase physical plastic Clipper cards at the airport or station kiosks, we will pay a $3.00 surcharge per card. That adds $42.00 in fees, bringing the grand total to $756.00. 
  > 
  > However, if we enroll scholars digitally and have them load a Clipper card onto their Apple Wallet or Google Wallet on their smartphones, the activation fee is completely waived. This drops our grand total to exactly $714.00, representing an instant $42 saving. I recommend we encourage scholars to use mobile wallets if they are comfortable with them."
